It's nice to meet you!

Hello, my name is Grace, and I am an electrical engineering major at the Georgia Institute of Technology, with planned threads in Circuit Technology and Electronic Devices. I am also pursuing a Computer Science minor in Computational Data Analysis.

Born and raised in Minnesota, I grew interested in electrical engineering at a young age. When I was nine, I curiously connected Christmas mini-LED electrodes to a nine-volt battery. With a POP! and a wisp of smoke, my interest in electrical engineering was sparked.

Throughout high school, I loved exploring STEM and was eager to gain a diverse set of experiences. I was the president and a founding member of my school's SWENext Club (Society of Women Engineers, Next Generation), where I worked to encourage students, especially females, to pursue careers in STEM. I was also the code subteam lead of my school's FIRST Robotics Competition Team, Team 2175, and ran varsity cross country and track and field, qualifying for multiple state championships.

Outside of school, I worked as a website technician at an application development and web design company. I also volunteered as the lead website adiminstrator for multiple nonprofit organizations, where I oversaw and trained staff and board members to maintan and update the website.

At Georgia Tech, I am a member of the Society of Women Engineers, as an extension of my SWENext experience, to promote gender diversity, remove barriers, and increase access to technology. I am also a part of Women in Electrical and Computer Engineering, and Georgia Tech Running Wreck, Georgia Tech's running club.

As a Georgia Tech engineer, I want to push the bounds of my creativity amd design for more sustainable, efficient, and accessible energy systems to be adopted by underserved communities. I hope to contribute towards diversity and accessibility in STEM and pioneer a more sustainable future.
